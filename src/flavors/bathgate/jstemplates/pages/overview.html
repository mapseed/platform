{{#_}}History{{/_}}

{{#_}}Formerly part of New Lots, East New York, has been home to waves of immigrant groups: Eastern Europeans in the 1800's and Puerto Ricans and African‐Americans from the south in the 1900's. Today it has a growing South Asian population. The garden lot was a former residence, transferred to the city in 1976. Adjacent buildings both constructed in 1901.{{/_}}

{{#_}}Climate forecasts{{/_}}

{{#_}}Rising temperatures{{/_}}

{{#_}}Mean annual temperatures are projected to increase by 4.1 to 5.7°F by the 2050s. The frequency of heat waves is projected to triple by the 2080s.{{/_}}

{{#_}}Rising sea levels and coastal flooding{{/_}}

{{#_}}Projections for sea level rise in New York City are 11 to 21inches by the 2050s, 18 to 39 inches by the 2080s, and could reach as high as 6 feet by 2100. Projected sea level rise will increase current 100-year coastal floods by 10-15 fold by the 2080s.{{/_}}

{{#_}}Increased precipitation{{/_}}

{{#_}}Mean annual precipitation is projected to increase by 4% to 11% by the 2050s.{{/_}}
